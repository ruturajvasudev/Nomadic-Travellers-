BRIEF REVIEW OF PREVIOUS CLASS (9:13 AM)

MAURYAN EMPIRE-(9:20 AM)

  • I] SIGNIFICANCE OF ASHOKAN EDICTS -
  • 1. ABOUT ASHOKAN -PERSONAL LIFE-
  • The names of his wives -were Devi(daughter of a great merchant -Vidhisha) and Karuvaki.
  • Devi is mentioned in an inscription found at Sanchi(Four lion capital).
  • His Dhammayatras -royal tours which he undertook to pave his respect to Buddha and visited almost all the major places associated with him including his birth place-Lumbini.
  • He also visited Khotan which is situated in central Asia and founded the city of Srinagar in Jammu and Kashmir.
  • Appointment of dhamma mahayatras- the officials responsible for the propagation of the principles of dhamma.
  • 2. SOME DETAILS RELATED TO ASHOKAN EDICTS  ABOUT MAURYAN BUREAUCRACY AND ADMINISTRATION-
  • Names of officials like- Mahamatras, Pradeshikas, Pradeshtris, Rajukas(judicial), Pulisini.
  • We also find some special measures taken by Asokha in some parts of his empire.
  • For example-
  • Reduction in the rate of land revenue at Lumbini as a mark of respect for Buddha. (the reduced rate is 1/8th)
  • Rumandevi inscriptions-classroom material.

LITERARY SOURCES of (9:54 AM)

  • RELIGIOUS LITERATURE-
  • Brahminical sources-
  • We find references to the Mauryan dynasty and its rulers in the Puranas dealing with ancient ruling dynasties and lineages.
  • But the Puranas (Vayu Puran, Matsya Puran etc) appeared to be quite hostile towards the Mauryans and described to them a low social origin.
  • Buddhist sources-
  • Buddhist sources particularly throw light on the rein of Ashok who is presented as a Monk in the garb of a king.
  • But his pre-Buddhist past is usually presented in dark sheds.
  • He is mentioned as a killer of 99 brothers and he also enjoyed torturing people in special torture chambers established by him.
  • He is given credit for constructing more than 80000 stupas which were later destroyed by anti-Buddhist ruler Pushyamitra Shunga.
  • Some important Buddhist texts through lights on the history of the Maurya empire-
  • Ashokavadana
  • Divyavadana
  • Srilankan chronicles like Dipvamsa, Mahavamsa, etc.
  • The Sri Lankan chronicles particularly highlight the efforts taken by Ashoka for the propagation of Buddhism in Sri lanka by sending his son Mahendra and daughter-Shangamitra.
  • Jain literature-
  • In the Jain religious literature, the founder of the Mauryan dynasty, Chandra Gupta Maurya is presented as a follower of Jainism.
  • Chandra Gupta Maurya abdicated his throne due to the conditions created by a long-drawn famine(12 years in Magadh).
  • After his abdication, he proceeded to the south along with his spiritual mentor Bhadrabahu.
  • In the south, he is said to have performed Sallekhana(Jain ritual-starvation till death) under the guidance of his guru(Bhadrabahu) at a place called Shravanbelagola(Karnataka).
  • Shravanbelagola is also known for the famous statue of Bahubali. (also known as Gomateshwar)
  • This statue was built by Chamuda Rai.
  • Bhadrabahu is also remembered as the founder of Digambara(sky-clad- Southerners).
  • Sthulabhadra-the founder of Svetambara.
  • SECULAR TEXTS-
  • Mudrarakshas-
  • A part of classical Sanskrit literature attributed to Vishakhadutt who belonged to a later time.
  • In this book we find all the legends related to the overthrow of the Nanda dynasty(Dhana Nanda) by Chandra Gupta Maurya and Chanakya.

ARTHASHASTRA (11:03 AM)

  • The Arthashastra, attributed to the legendary minister of Chandra Gupta Maurya, is considered one of the most important components of the secular and non-religious literary traditions of the sub-continent.
  • Its principles and postulates have been used to challenge many elements of the colonial perceptions related to Indian culture and civilisation.
  • The work deals with subjects like polity and statecraft which is written for Vijigishu. (the one who is desirous of conquest).
  • The work was first discovered in the year 1905 by Shama Shastri.
  • It was translated into English in 1915 and a critical edition of the work was published after independence by Kangley.
  • The authorship of the work is quite disputed as the names of its authors appear only through the Colophon of the text.
  • Similarly its chronology is quite confusing as based on the studies made in recent times, it appears to be an evolved text to which possibly many generations of scholars came to contribute.
  • Generally it is accepted that work belongs to the time of Mauryas.
  • And traits reflected in it are usually accepted as the realities of the Mauryan age.
  • However, quite ironically in the work we find no mention of the Mauryan dynasty or any of its rulers.
  • The Arthashastra throws light not only on politics and administration but also on some aspects of economic and social-cultural life.
  • Some of the important details related to the different aspects of life as reflected in the text are mentioned below-
  • Political-
  • Discussion on both principles and practices of politics and statecraft in a very pragmatic manner.
  • Chanakya is also known as Indian Machiavelli.
  • References to Saptanga(seven limbs)-theory of the state formation with the acceptance of the king as the most important limb of the state.
  • Discussion on the Mandala theory(circles of friend and foes)
  • The status, powers and privileges, duties and responsibilities and even day-to-day conduct of the affairs of the state in both the royal court and imperial household.
  • The king is placed at the top of both polity and society.
  • King has been placed even above the Dharmashastras as the order of the Rajagna.
  • Rajagna has been precedence over Shastric injunctions.
  • The king is vested with all the powers related to the state(executive, legislative and judicial).
  • Even the highest decision-making power -Parisha- seems to have just an advisory role.
  • The Parisha consists of the highest officials of the state possibly tirthas, who took their decisions based on the principle of majority and debates and discussions.
  • Since Parisha was neither a representative body nor the king was responsible towards it and so the Mauryan system represented a system of royal despotism.
  • The King is expected to rule in the interest of the ruled.
  • This challenged the theory of oriental despotism profounded by Western scholars.
  • A rigorous routine for the king dividing both the day and night into 8 parts each- known as Pahar(total 16 pahars).
  • Details related to different aspects of the Mauryan administration such as -
  • The revenue system, judicial system, military organisation and even the system of espionage(need to refer to the classroom material)
